## *Machine Learning for Human Flourishing*

This assignment challenges you to **design a machine learning application that genuinely helps people**. You will not build the model—instead, you will **think like a machine learning practitioner** and clearly explain how your idea would work.

# Goal

Design a **machine learning application that improves human flourishing**. The goal is to connect technical understanding with meaningful impact.

* Address a **real-world problem**
* Use **machine learning in a meaningful way** (not just for novelty)
* Show understanding of the **ML pipeline and tradeoffs**

# What to Submit

A **2–3 page written proposal** (clear, structured, and concise)

# Required Sections

## 1. The Idea (Motivation & Impact)

Describe your idea in plain language:

* What problem are you solving?
* Who does it help?
* Why does this matter for *human flourishing*?

Be specific. Avoid vague statements like “helps people live better.”

## 2. The Prediction Task

Define the ML problem clearly:

* What is the model predicting?

  * (e.g., classify, predict a number, recommend something)
* What are the **inputs (features)**?
* What is the **output (label/target)**?

## 3. The Data

Describe the data needed:

* Where would the data come from?
* What features would be important?
* Are there challenges?

  * Missing data?
  * Bias?
  * Privacy concerns?

## 4. The High-level Model

You do NOT need to write code, but you should:

* Suggest **1–2 models** that could work (e.g., logistic regression, decision tree, random forest)
* Explain **why** they are appropriate

## 5. Risks & Ethics

Discuss potential concerns:

* Could the model be biased?
* Could it be misused?
* What happens if it makes mistakes?

## 6. Human Flourishing Reflection

This is the heart of the assignment.

Reflect on:

* How does your system *actually improve people’s lives*?
* Does it empower people or replace them?
* Are there unintended consequences?

# Example Ideas (for inspiration)

* Early warning system for struggling students
* Mental health support recommendation system
* Personalized learning paths
* Fraud detection for vulnerable populations
* Health risk prediction for preventative care
* Energy usage optimization for families

# Common Pitfalls to Avoid

* “AI that helps everything” (too vague)
* No clear prediction target
* Ignoring data challenges
* Only focusing on accuracy, not consequences
* Treating ML as magic
