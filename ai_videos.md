## Give Me 18 Minutes and I’ll Make you Dangerously Smart (with AI)
https://youtu.be/A8_nNYLTXEQ?si=VJ5b39UscFqKyjP4

Synopsis:
This video outlines a four-step framework for using AI to **enhance** human intelligence rather than replacing it. The top 1% use AI as a training tool can outsmart complex situations.

### **The Four-Step Framework**

* **Step 1: Intelligent Laziness (The DRAG Framework):** Differentiate between "capped payoff" tasks (formatting, emails) and "uncapped payoff" tasks (strategy, product design). Use AI to **DRAG** through the mundane:
  * **D**rafting: Solve the blank page problem [[04:09](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=249)].
  * **R**esearch: Accelerate deep information gathering [[04:49](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=289)].
  * **A**nalysis: Find patterns in unstructured data [[05:40](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=340)].
  * **G**runt work: Reformatting, translating, and data cleaning [[06:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=362)].


* **Step 2: The Intelligent Hill (Prompting Mastery):** Move beyond "zero-shot" prompting (just asking for an answer) by climbing the hill of complexity:
  * **One-Shot/Few-Shot:** Provide examples to ground the model [[08:29](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=509)].
  * **Chain of Thought:** Force the AI to "think step by step" to reduce hallucinations [[09:56](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=596)].
  * **Agents:** Use agentic prompts to act as a multi-disciplinary team (e.g., researcher, analyst, and copywriter) [[10:45](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=645)].

* **Step 3: The Intelligent Gym (Progressive Overload):** Avoid "zero gravity" thinking by adding friction to learning. Use AI as a **spotter** to quiz you on concepts, applying "progressive overload" by asking increasingly difficult questions from high school to executive levels [[01:13:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=4430)], [[14:27](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=867)].

* **Step 4: The Intelligent Fool (The Beginner’s Mind):** Embrace the "fool's advantage" by admitting what you don't know. The video cites Microsoft’s 10x growth under Satya Nadella as a result of shifting from a "know-it-all" to a "learn-it-all" culture [[15:32](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=932)]. Use AI to ask the "dumb" questions you’d be too embarrassed to ask colleagues [[17:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=1022)].

### **Core Philosophy**

The central message is that intelligence is built through **resistance**, not convenience. While AI should be used to remove friction for informational tasks, it should be used to *add* friction for transformational tasks to prevent mental atrophy [[12:44](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=764)], [[13:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=802)].

## Example Prompts
The video provides several prompt examples categorized by their purpose in the "Intelligent Hill" and "Intelligent Gym" frameworks.

### **1. Drafting (AIM Protocol)**

Used to overcome the "blank page" problem for repetitive tasks.

* **Prompt:** "Hey AI, act in this [Role], use this [Input], and this is your [Mission]." [[04:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=257)]

### **2. One-Shot Prompting**

Providing a single example to guide style and tone.

* **Prompt:** "Write a LinkedIn post about remote work. Use this specific post as a style guide: [Paste Post Here]." [[08:36](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=516)]

### **3. Few-Shot Prompting & Grounding**

Providing multiple examples to establish patterns and avoid hallucinations.

* **Prompt:** "Here are five of my previous presentations: [Attach/Paste Docs]. Now write a new presentation based on my tone of voice on topic XYZ." [[09:28](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=568)]
* **Pro Tip:** Follow up by asking: "Explain the pattern back to me first." [[09:36](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=576)]

### **4. Chain of Thought Reasoning**

Forcing the AI to explain its logic before providing a final answer.

* **Prompt:** "Do not refine my research report yet. List the top three most impactful areas of improvement after we analyze it. Tell me why you think so and suggest how we address each. Think step by step. Show me your thinking for each step." [[10:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=622)]

### **5. Agentic Prompts**

Assigning multiple roles or complex, multi-step tasks to a single prompt.

* **Prompt:** "Do deep research on trends on topic XYZ. Analyze and cross-reference all the trends to find the three most important ones, and draft a one-page memo summarizing the findings." [[11:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=667)]

### **6. Progressive Overload (The Intelligent Gym)**

Using AI as a "spotter" to master a concept through increasingly difficult challenges.

* **Initial Step:** Paste a concept text and prompt: "I need to master this concept. Quiz me on it." [[14:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=853)]
* **Level 1:** "Quiz me like I am a high school student." [[14:32](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=872)]
* **Level 2:** "Ask me questions like I am a college student." [[14:35](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=875)]
* **Level 3:** "Grill me like you’re interviewing me for an executive job." [[14:38](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=878)]
* **Level 4:** "Challenge me like an irate boss who thinks I’m unprepared." [[14:42](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=882)]

### **7. Beginner’s Mind (Simplification)**

Using AI to learn complex topics from scratch.

* **Prompt:** "Pick one thing that you don’t understand in your field... can you explain it to me in a simpler way? Teach me like I am 10 years old." [[17:15](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A8_nNYLTXEQ&t=1035)]
